Research context
Vision

To make Australia's transport networks among the most intelligent and responsive in the world, where research and operations advance together rather than apart.

Mission

The bridge between transport research and live network operations, building data infrastructure, algorithms, and decision-support tools grounded in operational reality.

The research problems
▲
Boundary fragmentation. Road and public transport planning run in separate silos. Motorway and arterial control systems are uncoordinated. Network instabilities propagate across boundaries no single agency owns.
◆
Tacit knowledge loss. Decades of operational expertise held by experienced engineers with no scalable transfer mechanism. As the workforce turns over, institutional network intelligence is lost.
■
Research-to-operations gap. Strong transport research exists but the pathway from publication to operational deployment is slow, costly, and rarely built in from the start.

Research methodology
Operationally co-designed from day one.
01
Problem definition with operators
Co-defined with operational partners; grounded in what actually constrains network performance.
02
Fit-for-purpose experimental design
Developed and tested on real operational sites or datasets with operators embedded during testing.
03
Honest validation, null results included
Null results are valid contributions. Rigour over optimism is a cultural principle.
04
Deployment path designed in from day one
Implementation pathway planned before research starts. What gets built is built to deploy.
The operating model

Layer 1: Connected multimodal data foundation (STREAMS, GLiVe, Bluetooth, AFC). Layer 2: Proactive algorithm layer crossing the motorway-arterial boundary. Layer 3: InteliAgent agentic AI decision support translating live data and institutional knowledge into operator intelligence.
